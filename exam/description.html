Main Goal: Visualize ghettos in Denmark

Since 2010 the Danish government has published a list of "vulnerable housing projects" (or "særligt udsatte boligområder" in Danish) also known as the "ghetto list". In short, if a housing area (which is slightly losely defined) which satisfies 4 out of 5 of a list of criteria then that area is considered a "ghetto".

The ghetto list ends up being a frame of reference for public discourse, but it is not at the forefront exactly what it means to be a ghetto in Denmark. Through visualization, we wish to give an intuitive idea of what it actually means to be a Danish ghetto.

Subgoals:

Here's a rough sketch of some of the things we would like to visualize:
  1. Present an overview of ghettos. How much of an outlier are they? Where are the ghettos located geograhically?
  2. Compare ghettos with their respective municipality. How big is the difference? How does having a ghetto affect a municipality
  3. What has been the evolution in ghettos in the period 2010-2017. How is the gap between ghettos and Danish municipalities moving?

Where Our Data Is From:

The ghetto list is published as a PDF which contains the criteria for the respective housing areas. We will have to transcipe a data set from this series of PDFs.

For municipal data, we will make use of Statistikbanken which is published by Danmark's Statistik.
