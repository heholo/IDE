The Danish Ghettos

The topic of danish ghettos and the yearly released ghettolist has become a hot topic in Denmark ever since prime minister Lars Løkke Rasmussen held his new years speech, where the main topic were the ghettos.

Criticism has been raised against the image of danish ghettos that Lars Løkke paint in his speech, such as in these articles:
  • Økonom om Løkkes ghettosnak: Det er totalt ude af trit med virkeligheden
  • Ghettoliste: Bispeparken kunne have været fjernet fra omstridt ghettoliste
The yearly released ghettolist ( Ghettolist 2017 ) contains a list of subsidized housing areas (almene boligområder) with more than 1000 residents, that fulfill 3 out of the 5 criteria. Interpreting these numbers without anything to compare with is a challenge. In the following visualizations the danish municipalities have been evaluated over the same 5 criteria and shown alongside the ghettos for context.

Criteria Definitions

To be categorized as a ghetto a subsidized housing area (alement boligområde) need more than 1000 residents and fulfill 3 out of these 5 criteria:

  1. Unemployed: The percentage of residents between age 18-64 not studying or working is greater than 40% (Averaged over last two years).
  2. Immigrants: The percentage of immigrants and descendants from non-western countries is greater than 50%
  3. Prosecuted: The percentage of residents over 18 persecuted with the danish penal code (straffelov), weapon law (vågenlov) or drug law (lov om euforiserende stoffer) is greater than 2.7% (Averaged over last two years).
  4. Education: Percentage of residents between age 30-59 that only has an elementary level education (including uninformed) is greater than 50%
  5. Income: The average gross income for taxable residents between age 15-64 (excluding students) are less than 55% of the same average for the region the housing area is located in.
